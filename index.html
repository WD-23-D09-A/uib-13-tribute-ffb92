BMW E36

the Evolution of the E30

"For sheer driving pleasure"

  • The BMW E36, the third generation of the 3 Series, was produced from 1990 to 1999.
  • Available in sedan, coupe, convertible, and wagon variants, the E36 catered to a diverse customer base.
  • Offered a range of engines, including four-cylinder and six-cylinder variants, allowing customers to choose their preferred level of power.
  • Introduced the M3 variant, featuring a potent inline-six engine and upgraded suspension components, appealing to motorsport enthusiasts.
  • Noted for its sleek and aerodynamic body design, the E36 set new standards in automotive aesthetics during its time.
  • The E36's success solidified BMW's reputation for building engaging and well-engineered sports sedans, establishing the 3 Series as a benchmark in the automotive industry.

The BMW E36 3 Series was the first BMW model to introduce a hatchback variant, known as the 3 Series Compact. This innovative design provided a sporty yet practical option for customers who desired the handling and performance of a BMW coupled with the versatility of a hatchback.
